Sahutchai Inwongwan

Scientist · Biologist · Biochemist
Lecturer in Systems Biology, Dept. of Biology, Chiang Mai University, Thailand
Honorary Research Fellow, GEE, University College London, UK
DPhil, Dept. of Plant Sciences, University of Oxford, UK

Sahutchai's DPhil at Oxford explored the capacities of the Euglena metabolic network using metabolic flux analysis. He subsequently held a research fellowship at University College London studying mitonuclear incompatibility in Drosophila, before returning to Chiang Mai University to establish the Systems Integration Laboratory.

Research

Research Areas

Our lab operates at the intersection of experimental biology and computational modelling, tackling fundamental and applied questions across six interconnected themes.

🌿
Euglena gracilis — Metabolic Modelling
Genome-scale metabolic modelling and network optimisation for carbon capture, beta-glucan production, shikimate-derived products, and industrial biofactory applications.
⚡
Organelle Bioenergetics
Mitochondria–chloroplast coordination in Euglena; bioenergetic synergy, metabolic adaptation, and mitonuclear interactions in Drosophila.
🌫️
PM2.5 & Lung Cancer
Multi-omics analysis of biomass-haze PM2.5 exposure in non-small cell lung cancer cells; oxidative stress, transcriptomic remodelling, and therapy resistance.
🚀
Astrobiology & Astrobioinnovation
Genome-scale metabolic modelling of extremophiles; physiology of organisms in space-analog conditions and its biotechnological applications.
🍯
Fermentation Science
Physicochemical and biological characterisation of fermented beverages (mead); new fermentation processes and microbial strategies for high-value products.
🐝
Honeybee Microbiome
Metabolic modelling of honeybee gut microbiome; leveraging gut symbionts for pollinator health, bee growth, and bioresource potential.
